Hi! I am Katie Barr, a current art student and multi-media artist.

To me, creating art is one of the most vital things in my life. Whether it be in the margins of my notes, in a sketchbook, or drawn into my desk, I, like many other artists, am itching to create something. While I donâ€™t consider myself to be proficient in any specific field, I enjoy exploring many mediums and medias. So, though digital art is a new territory for me, it has been exciting and fun to explore. I previously have worked mainly in traditional mediums, particularly painting and drawing, but have found new interest in exploring digital art as well as returning to photography and videography, which I previously did a lot of.

My art is inspired by my personal life and loved ones, the nature and environment around me, and cartoon and animation art styles. Animation styles are very inspiring to me, and I am often inspired by the character designs and coloring styles of various animations. (I remember when Ponyo came out I would ask for the concept art book for it for it, and now I am pursuing art and considering following a career path into concept art.) My love for animation has pushed me to explore vibrant and representational colors and dynamic poses. Animation being an important part of my art-inspiration and life as a whole is what sparked and sustained my interest in art from a younger age to now.

In painting, my subjects are typically more personal, being my family and loved ones, and close-personal subject matters. I have been more recently inspired by surrealist art as well, creating pieces that are based more in the subconscious and dream-like aspects of the mind. The colors and other-worldly-like atmosphere of surrealism have inspired my own representations of the world in my art. I have used this to tackle person subject matter in my works and show how my mind perceives the world.
